Procurement

Procurement & Tendering

Data center projects involve many specialist subcontractors. We help clients establish rigorous specifications during tendering and procurement, so future construction and commissioning have a clear basis — greatly reducing the risk of change orders and budget overruns.

01 Planning 02 Procurement 03 Construction 04 Commissioning 05 Operations
Our Principle
"The key to avoiding misalignment during execution is to write testing requirements, document deliverables, responsibility boundaries, and system interfaces fully into the contract — at the procurement stage."
1

Defining Procurement Specifications

We translate detailed design precisely into executable, measurable specifications (RFP/RFQ), so every bidder shares a fully consistent understanding of the engineering standards.

2

Vendor Selection Strategy

We help clients build scientific evaluation criteria, with strict scrutiny and due diligence on vendors' track record, technical capability, financial standing, and HSE standards.

3

Defining Responsibility Boundaries & Interfaces

Grey areas in multi-contract packages are where blame-shifting happens. Before awarding, we clearly demarcate the responsibility boundaries between M&E, HVAC, ELV, and fire-protection systems.

4

Embedding Testing & Delivery Requirements Early

Breaking the "figure out testing after it's built" mindset. Contracts mandate the five-stage Commissioning (Cx) obligations, QA/QC requirements, and as-built document delivery standards.
