2. Procurement & Tendering

Procurement & Tendering

"The key to avoiding cognitive gaps during execution lies in the procurement and tendering phase: explicitly integrating testing requirements, document deliverables, responsibility boundaries, and system interfaces into the contract specifications."

Data center projects involve numerous specialized subcontractors. We assist clients in establishing rigorous specifications during the bidding and procurement phase, ensuring future construction and acceptance are well-documented, significantly reducing the risks of project changes and budget overruns.

Clarifying Procurement Specifications

Accurately translating preliminary detailed designs into actionable and measurable Request for Proposals/Quotations (RFP/RFQ), ensuring all bidders have a completely consistent understanding of engineering standards.

Vendor Selection Strategy

Assisting clients in formulating scientific evaluation metrics, conducting strict gatekeeping and due diligence on suppliers' past performance, technical capabilities, financial status, and Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) standards.

Establishing Boundaries & Interfaces

In multi-package contracts, grey areas are most prone to buck-passing. Before tendering, we clearly define the Demarcation Points between mechanical, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, ELV, and fire protection systems.

Early Inclusion of Testing & Delivery

Breaking the traditional mindset of "thinking about how to test only after building." We mandatorily include coordination obligations for the five phases of Commissioning (Cx), QA/QC requirements, and as-built documentation delivery standards in the contract.
